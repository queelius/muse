# Optimization pass: triage verdict (baseline phase)

Box: Ryzen 5 7430U laptop (6C/12T, freq-scaling) for LLM; frodo (RTX 3060
12GB + 4-core i5-4460) for modalities. Same-GGUF head-to-head:
muse `qwen2.5-3b-instruct-gguf-q4-k-m` vs ollama `qwen2.5:3b-instruct`
(both Q4_K_M).

## LLM verdict: NO fix clears the 20% bar -- muse is already competitive

| Path | muse | ollama | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| non-streaming tok/s (wall) | 10.9-15.7 | 7.8-8.6 | muse FASTER |
| streaming tok/s (alternating, committed json) | 5.8-14.3 | 7.6-10.9 | INCONCLUSIVE: muse variance (2.5x within one run) exceeds the muse-ollama delta; no consistent >=20% deficit demonstrated |
| gateway hop overhead | 0.7% non-stream, ~6% stream | n/a | exonerated |
| prompt-prefix/KV reuse | works (1418-tok re-eval ~free; flat turns) | works | parity |
| n_threads default | 6 (= physical cores, llama-cpp default) | 6 (physical) | matched by construction |

The originally perceived "muse much slower than ollama" did NOT reproduce
on same-model/same-quant hardware. Streaming comparisons on this box are noise-dominated (laptop thermal
scaling + concurrent workloads): an early-run 3x penalty and a later
apparent parity both dissolved under repetition. The committed
alternating run (llm-streaming-alternating.json) is the evidence of
record. An early-run 3x streaming penalty
turned out to be transient window contention (laptop thermal scaling +
concurrent workload): the identical scenario measured 4.5 tok/s in one
window and 15.9 in another. Lesson recorded: single-window medians on
this box are not comparable across windows; the harness's alternating
same-window pattern is the trustworthy protocol.

Likely source of the original perception: cross-model comparisons (e.g.
the 32B at ~1.3 tok/s in muse vs smaller models in ollama), or a
contended window.
